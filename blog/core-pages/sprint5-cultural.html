Core learnings during Foundations

  • What are three new things you have learned about yourself and your ego as a result of the core learning?

    I don’t think I learnt new things about myself, instead the core learning just confirmed them: I am proud, every time I don't understand or I can't do something, it's a defeat for me; I found myself working really hard as usual, but during this foundation more than ever. Maybe a new thing I learned about myself is that I'm much more determined than I expected.

  • What has surprised you the most about the core learning?

    I found really surprising how your personal behavior toward a problem/challenge has a big influence on the final result, how this can determine if you have a growth mindset or a fixed one. Usually when I find myself facing a difficult problem I always start with "I'll never make it", but thanks to the research on growth mindset and neuroplasticity I changed my attitude towards the problem by repeating to myself "I can do it".

  • What were the most challenging aspects of the core learning?

    Doing the research about each argument of every Sprint and think about how you could have “applied” what you learned to yourself.

  • Why do you think we, a programming school, are spending so much time focusing on core learning in a web development bootcamp course?

    Since during a bootcamp you work with other people, I think that is really important for yourself to understand who you are, what your skills are, your attitude towards a problem, how you interact with other people, how you handle your relationship with others.

  • Does the time you spent studying core learning here feel like a waste of time? Should you have just used that time to practicing programming instead? Justify your answer.

    I do not consider the Core part a waste of time, I learned a lot of useful things, but the research and reflection parts took time in each Sprint, time that I personally would have preferred to use in studying and practicing programming. Perhaps a week, for both the core and the technical sections of each sprint, is not enough.
