IQ & EQ


What is Emotional Intelligence?

Daniel Goleman, psychologist, lecturer, and science journalist and also author of Emotional Intelligence, defines Emotional Intelligence (EQ) as the ability to identify, assess, and control one’s own emotions, the emotions of others, and that of groups.
In other words is the ability to recognize and understand emotions in yourself and others, and use this awareness to manage your behavior and relationships.

There are four fundamental aspects of EQ:

  • Self-awareness : it's about recognizing your emotions. When people with a Low EQ get angry or sad they will not look at why they are feeling in that way : usually they blame others, or become depressed or bullies. Oppositely, people with High EQ try to understand why their are feeling that way, what’s causing it and they will try to get better.

  • Self-management : it’s about how you manage yourself. People with a Low EQ that become angry or sad, will shout at other people or start a fight, or they redirect their emotions in a self-destructive way. People with High EQ instead, will try to manage their emotions, take actions. For example they would deal with a stressed situation by working-out or meeting people that can cheer them up. up

  • Social-awareness : this skill is about treating people according to their emotional reactions. A person with a Low EQ is not able to see social cues and micro behaviors of others, it usually goes wherever the wind takes him/her and sometimes other people can take advantage of that. On the contrary, a person with an High EQ listens to others and put words in context, ask questions about the other people, read between the lines.

  • Social-management : it's about managing relationships, finding common ground and feeling a rapport with others. A person with a Low EQ has problems finding friends or like-minded people, or getting its point during a conversation. It often feels misunderstood and it will blame it on superficial things (for example not enough money). A person with a High EQ uses its intuitions and adapts if necessary, find a win-win situation, common ground in order to connect with another person.
How is it different to IQ?

IQ (or Intelligence Quotient):

  • is a number/score derived from a standardized intelligence test;
  • is used to determine academic abilities and identify individuals with intelligence above the average;
  • is an inborn ability;
  • ensures success in School.

EQ

  • is a learned and improved ability;
  • in the workplace, is it used to identify leaders and good team players;
  • ensures success in life.

Why is it important?

EQ is important because it helps you to develop significant human relationships: it determines how you interact and connect with others, and how do you treat people in your life.
It also assists you in looking after your physical and mental health and wellbeing: how do you cope in a situation of anger or pressure or how do you face the crisis.
Micheal Benner, seminar leader, speaker, coach & author of Fearless Intelligence, during his talk at TEDx 2011 started his presentation with this question:
"If success doesn’t create happiness and love – what does?"
He states that each individual is responsible for love and happiness in his own life, achievable through self-empowerment and emotional intelligence. He emphasizes the importance of always taking responsibility for our emotions, regardless of the way they’re effected by other people, and being empatich and caring about others.

"Success does not create happiness and love, happiness and love is the way to success."
